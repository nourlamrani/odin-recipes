Rechta Algerian Noodles Served with Broth

DESCRIPTION

Rechta is a special dish in Algerian cuisine. It is enjoyed by families across the country at all times of the year. It is a typical Algerian food. The noodles are cooked in a white sauce, seasoned with cinnamon. It is served with chicken and vegetables, usually turnip and zucchini. Algerians generally prepare the noodles with homemade dough made from durum wheat semolina, flour, and egg. It is easy to make, wholesome, and flavorful. Algerians commonly serve this dish for special events, such as El Mouloud, Achoura, and El Eid.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 kg of homemade rechta pasta or store-bought noodles
  • A free-range chicken and 2 nice pieces of lamb meat
  • 1 bowl of chickpeas, soaked overnight
  • 4 or 5 turnips
  • 2 onions
  • 50 g of butter
  • oil or semen
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Eggs for decoration
  • Cinnamon powder
    •

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. In a pot, brown with oil or smen, chicken, lamb with minced onion, spices, cinnamon stick.
  2. when the juices are in the bottom of the pot, pour about 1.5 liters of warm water.
  3. Add the soaked and rinsed chickpeas.
  4. When the chicken is cooked, remove the pot and close for 10 to 15 minutes to finish cooking the lamb and chickpeas.
  5. When the lamb meat is well cooked just melting, remove it and add the turnips cut fairly large.
  6. Check the seasoning, add water if necessary.
